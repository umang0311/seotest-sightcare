Lutein:  

Lutein is a natural yellow pigment known as a carotenoid. It's often found in high amounts in certain fruits and vegetables. When we eat lutein, our bodies can turn it into a useful form that works inside our eye cells. Its job is to shield our eyes from damage caused by harmful substances called free radicals.

By stopping these free radicals before they can cause any real harm, lutein helps keep our eyes healthy for longer than they would be without this protective effect.

Omega-3 Fatty Acids:

Omega-3 fatty acids, such as DHA and EPA, are important fats that have a big impact on your eye health. Omega-3s can also lessen inflammation, making your eyes feel better and less dry.

Vitamins A, C, and E:

These vitamins are like protective shields for your eyes. They act as antioxidants, guarding your eyes from harm caused by free radicals.

Zeaxanthin:

Zeaxanthin is one of the two special pigments in the back of your eye, in a spot called the macula. It's like a natural shield for your vision.


Zeaxanthin helps your macula absorb blue light from the sun and other sources, so the important cells in your eye can do their job. It also helps make the pictures on your retina by gathering light from different angles.

Besides that, zeaxanthin acts as a strong protector, fighting off inflammation and harmful stuff called free radicals near your eyes. This helps prevent issues like cataracts in the lens and diseases like age-related macular degeneration (AMD) in your retina.
